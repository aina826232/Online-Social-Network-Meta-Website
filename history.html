History of Meta

Creation of Facebook

Facebook was created in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg and his co-founders at Harvard University. Initially designed as a social network for college students, it quickly expanded into a global platform with billions of users. Facebook became a symbol of social networking success but also faced numerous controversies, including concerns over user privacy and the spread of misinformation.

The Cambridge Analytica Data Scandal (2018)

In 2018, Facebook was embroiled in the Cambridge Analytica scandal, where it was revealed that the data of millions of users had been improperly harvested and used to influence elections. This incident sparked a global outcry, led to significant regulatory fines, and brought CEO Mark Zuckerberg to testify at numerous government hearings. The scandal severely impacted Facebook’s reputation and trust among users.

Meta’s Rebranding (2021)

In October 2021, Facebook, Inc. rebranded itself as Meta Platforms, Inc. The rebranding aimed to highlight the company’s commitment to the metaverse, a virtual world where people can interact, work, and play. It also served to rehabilitate its image after years of controversies. The name “Meta” reflects its focus on virtual and augmented reality technologies, signaling a shift beyond traditional social media

CEO Mark Zuckerberg’s Metaverse Vision

At the 2021 Connect conference, Meta’s CEO Mark Zuckerberg declared the metaverse to be the successor to the mobile internet. He envisioned a fully immersive virtual space where users could socialize, work, and engage in experiences beyond the physical world. This vision drives Meta’s investments in VR/AR technologies, including products like Meta Quest and platforms like Horizon Worlds.

Meta’s Portfolio

Today, Meta is the parent company of major platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger. It continues to shape the tech landscape through its ambitious metaverse projects while navigating challenges related to privacy, regulation, and competition.
